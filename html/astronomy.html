Some unit conversions

  • arc min - ' - $\frac{1}{60}$ of 1 degree
    • note: if 1 deg is $\frac{1}{360}$ of a turn, one arc min is $\frac{1}{21600}$
  • arc sec - " - $\frac{1}{3600}$ of a degree

The Magnitude Scale

  • Apparent Magnitude - how bright a star appears in the sky. m = 1 for brightest and m = 6 for dimmest.
  • Flux - (or radiant flux, $F$) the total amount of light energy of all wavelengths per unit time. J/s
  • Luminosity - Energy emitted per second
  • Flux and Luminosity are related by $F = \frac{L}{4\pi r^2}$
  • Absolute magnitude, $M$
    • Defined as the apparent magnitude of a star if the observer were located at a distance of 10 pc
    • $m_1 - m_2 = -2.5 \mathrm{log}_{10}(\frac{F_1}{F_2})$
    • The stars distance is given by $d = 10^{(m-M+5)/5}$
  • Distance modulus: $m-M = 5\mathrm{log}_{10}(d)-5 = 5\mathrm{log}_{10}\frac{d}{10\mathrm{pc}}$
  • Relation between absolute magnitude and luminosity $m=M_{Sun}-2.5\mathrm{log}_{10}(\frac{L}{L_\odot})$

Initial Mass Function(IMF)

  • The distribution of stellar masses in a large cluster of stars
  • Crossing time: The time required for a star or galaxy to move from one side of a gravitationally bound system to another.
  • Observations suggest that star formation occurs in only one or two crossing times for a range of scales spanning a factor of ~1000.
    • This is considered a short period for the rate of formation of stars.
  • Observations in the Orion Nebula Cluster(ONC) determine otherwise.
