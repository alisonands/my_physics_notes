  1. In a nonrelativistic, one-dimensional collision, a particle of mass 2m collides with a particle of mass m at rest. If the particles stick together after the collision, what fraction of the initial kinetic energy is lost in the collision?
    2. Sol Conservation of momentum:
    $p_i = 2mv_i$
    $p_f = 3mv_f$
    $\therefore \, v_i = \frac{3}{2}v_f$
    Conservation of energy:
    $K_i = \frac{1}{2}(2m)v_i^2 = mv_i^2$
    $K_f = \frac{1}{2}(3m)v_f^2 = \frac{3}{2}mv_f^2 $
    Sub in $v_i = \frac{3}{2}v_f$
    $K_i = \frac{1}{2}(2m)\left(\frac{3}{2}v_f\right)^2$
    $K_i = \frac{9}{2}mv_f^2$

    Fraction of kinetic energy lost:
    $\frac{K_f}{K_i} = \frac{3/2}{9/2} = \frac{1}{3}$ $\therefore$ The fraction of kinetic energy lost in the collision is 1/3.

  2. A three-dimensional harmonic oscillator is in thermal equilibrium with a temperature reservoir at temperature T. The average total energy of the oscillator is
    3. Sol

  3. Two stars are separated by an angle of $3\times 10^{-5}$ radians. What is the diameter of the smallest telescope that can resolve two stars using visible light ($\lambda$ ≅ 600 nanometers)? (Ignore any effects due to Earth's atmosphere.)
    Sol The Rayleigh criteriion is the generally accepted criterion for the minimum resolvable detail.
    Single slit: $\mathrm{sin}\theta_R = \frac{\lambda}{d}$
    Circular aperture: $\mathrm{sin}\theta_R = 1.22\frac{\lambda}{d}$
    In this case, a circular aperture gives $d = \frac{1.22\lambda}{\theta} = \frac{1.22\times 600 \times 10^{-9}}{3\times 10^{-5}} = 0.0244$m

  5. An 8-centimeter-diameter by 8-centimeter-long NaI(TI) detector detects gamma rays of a specific energy from a point source of radioactivity. When the source is placed just next to the detector at the center of the circular face, 50 percent of all emitted gamma rays at that energy are detected. If the detector is moved to 1 meter away, the fraction of detected gamma rays drops to
    Sol Power, Intensity, Area $P = IA$
    If there is no power loss, $P_1 = P_2$, $I_1A_1 = I_2A_2 \rightarrow \frac{I_1}{I_2} = \frac{A_2}{A_1} = \frac{\pi r_1^2}{4\pi r_2^2}$
    The fraction of gamma rays detected is the ratio of surface area of the detector to the ratio of the surface area of the sphere at 1m.
    So $\frac{(4\times 10^{-2})^2}{1} = 4\times 10^{-4}$m.

  7. The energy required to remove both electrons from the Helium atom in its ground state is 79.0 eV. How much energy is required to ionize Helium (i.e. to remove one electron)?
    Sol $E_n = \frac{13.6 Z^2}{n^2}$
    For helium, $Z = 2$
    n is the ground state n = 1
    $E_1 = 13.6\times 4 = 54.4$eV. This is the energy to remove the second electron.
    So the energy needed to remove one electron is $79-54.4 = 24.6$eV

  8. In the hydrogen spectrum, the ratio of the wavelength for Lyman-radiation (n = 2 to n = 1) to Balmer-radiation (n = 3 to n = 2) is
    Sol
    Rydberg formula:
    $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H\left(\frac{1}{n_1^2}-\frac{1}{n_2^2}\right)$
    Lynman:
    $\frac{1}{\lambda_L} = R_H\left(\frac{1}{1^2}-\frac{1}{2^2}\right)$
    $\lambda_L = \frac{4}{3R_H}$
    Balmer:
    $\frac{1}{\lambda_B} = R_H\left(\frac{1}{2^2}-\frac{1}{3^2}\right)$
    $\lambda_B = \frac{36}{5R_H}$
    $\frac{\lambda_L}{\lambda_B} = \frac{5}{27}$

  10. A free particle with initial kinetic energy E and de Broglie wavelength $\lambda$ enters a region in which it has potential energy $V$. What is the particle's new de Broglie wavelength?
    Sol
    The DeBroglie wavelength of a free particle: $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$
    The kinetic energy of a free particle: $E = \frac{p^2}{2m} \rightarrow p = \sqrt{2mE}$
    The momentum of a particle with potential energy V: $p = \sqrt{2m(E-V)}$
    So the wavelength of the particle is $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m(E-V)}} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mE-2mV}} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mE(1-E/V)}} = \lambda \left(1-E/V\right)^{-1}$

  11. A sealed and thermally insulated container of total volume V is divided into two equal volumes by an impermeable wall. The left half of the container is initially occupied by n moles of a ideal gas at temperature T. Which of the following gives the change in entropy of the system when the wall is suddenly removed and the gas expands to fill the entire volume?
    Sol

    $Nk = nR --> S = nR\mathrm{ln}2$

  13. At 20°C, a pipe open at both ends resonates at a frequency of 440 hertz. At what frequency does the same pipe resonate on a particularly cold day when the speed of sound is 3 percent lower than it would be at 20°C ?
    Sol
    If the temperature decreases, the frequency decreases.
    $v = \frac{\lambda}{T} = \frac{\lambda}f \rightarrow \frac{f}{f'} = \frac{v}{(1-0.3)v}$
    $f' = f \times 0.97 = 440(0.97) = 426.8$

  14. The first filter always reduces the intensity of the light to half, I1=½ I0 The next filter reduces the intensity by In = I(n-1) cos2 θ θ is the angle with respect to the nth filter.
    Sol

    The other filters reduce light by $I = I_0 \mathrm{cos}^2\theta$ so the second filter gives
    $I = \frac{I_0}{2} \mathrm{cos}^2(45) = \frac{1}{4} I_0$

  16. The Lyman alpha spectral line of Hydrogen (λ = 122 nanometers) differs by $1.8\times 10^{-12}$ meter in spectra taken at opposite ends of the Sun's equator. What is the speed of a particle on the equator due to the Sun's rotation, in kilometers per second?

    $v = \frac{(3\times 10^8)(1.8\times 10^{-12})}{(1.22\times 10^{-7})} \approx 2.2$km/s
