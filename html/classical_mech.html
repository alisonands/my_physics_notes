Projectile Motion and Drag

  • Air resistance, or drag, can be written as $f = bv + cv^2 + ...$
  • for linear drag, we have $\vec{f} = -b\vec{v}$, and the equation of motion is therefore $m\vec{a} = m\vec{g}-b{\vec{v}}$
  • Terminal speed is given when $m\vec{a} = 0$ and is $v_{ter} = \frac{mg}{b}$, and for the quadratic case, $v_{ter} = \sqrt{\frac{mg}{c}}$

Energy and Work

  • Kinetic energy: $\frac{1}{2}mv^2$
  • Work: $\int F \cdot dr$
  • Potential energy: $\Delta U = -\int_a^b F(r)\,dr = -W$
    • Gravity: $U = mgh$
    • Spring: $U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$
  • Power: $P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \vec{F}\cdot \vec{v}$
  • For conservative forces, stokes theorem gives $W = \oint F \cdot \, dr = 0$

Center of mass

  • $R_{CM} = \frac{1}{M} \sum_{i=1}^n m_ir_i=\frac{1}{M}\int r\,dm$

Some rotational variables

  • $\theta = \frac{s}{r}$
  • $\omega = \frac{d\theta}{dt}$
  • $\alpha = \frac{d\omega}{dt}$

Parallel Axis theorem

  • $I = I_{CM}+Mh^2$

Torque

  • $\tau = r \times F = I\alpha$
  • The work and power of torque can be written as
    $W = \int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2} \tau \, d\theta$
    and
    $P = \tau \cdot \omega$

Angular momentum

  • ${L} = r \times p = m(r \times v)$
  • $\tau = \frac{dL}{dt}$

Newton's law of gravitation

  • $F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$

Bernouilli's

  • $P+\rho gy+ \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 = \mathrm{const}$

Oscillations

  • $F = m\ddot{x} = -kx$
  • Second order differential equation
    $\ddot{x}+\omega x^2 = 0$
  • The period of a spring
    $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$
  • The period of a simple pendulum
    $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}$
  • The solution(s) are as follows
    $x(t) = A cos (\omega t - \phi)$
    $v(t) = -A\omega sin (\omega t - \phi)$
    $a(t) = A\omega^2 cos (\omega t -\phi)$

Damped Oscillations

  • Adding a damped term $-b\dot{x}$
    $F = m\ddot{x} = -kx-b\dot{x}$
  • Second order differential equation
    $m\ddot{x}+b\dot{x}+kx = 0$
  • Setting $\beta = \frac{b}{2m}$ and $\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$, the differential equation becomes
    $\ddot{x} + 2\beta\dot{x} + \omega_0^2 x = 0$
