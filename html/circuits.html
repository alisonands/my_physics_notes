Circuits

  • $I_{rms} = \frac{I_0}{\sqrt{2}}$
  • $V_{rms} = \frac{V_0}{\sqrt{2}}$
  • $V = IR$

AC circuits

Inductive circuit

  • Consist of inductor L and AC power source
  • Acts like resistance, given by $X_L = 2\pi f L$
  • The voltage is given by $V = IX_L$
  • high frq, less current & vice versa
  • Inductor stores energy
  • frq --> number of times signal changes direction per second

Capacitive circuit

  • $X_c = \frac{1}{2\pi fc} = \frac{1}{\omega c}$
  • $V = IX_c$
  • Capacitor also stores energy like an inductor
  • only resistors dissipate energy

Impedence

  • For an RLC circuit, impedence is $Z = \sqrt{R^2+{X_L^2-X_C^2}}$
  • Total current in RLC circuit: $V = IZ$
  • phase angle: $\theta = \mathrm{tan}^{-1}\left(\frac{X_L-X_C}{R}\right)$
  • Power: $P = I^2R$
  • Average power: $\bar{P} = I_{rms}V_{rms}cos\phi$

Resonant frequency

  • of LC or RLC circuits: $f_R = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}$
  • At resonant frequency, $X_C = X_L$, and $Z = R$
